Introduction

Bees are vital pollinators, ensuring the reproduction of plants and contributing to global food security[2]. They are responsible for pollinating crops such as almonds, apples, and tomatoes[3].

Why Bees Matter

Pollination by bees increases crop yield and quality.

Pollination by bees increases crop yield and quality. For example, blueberries and cherries are 90% dependent on honey bee pollination, while almonds rely entirely on honey bees.

Bees also support biodiversity by enabling plants to reproduce, which sustains ecosystems and wildlife habitats.

Threats to Bees

Bee populations face challenges like habitat loss and climate change.

Bee populations face challenges like habitat loss, climate change, and pesticide use. These factors threaten their survival and the ecosystems they support[4].

Climate change is particularly harmful as it disrupts the synchrony between bees and flowering plants, reducing forage availability[4]. Habitat fragmentation further isolates bee populations, increasing extinction risks.

How to Help

Plant pollinator-friendly gardens with native flowers.

  • Plant pollinator-friendly gardens with native flowers.
  • Support local beekeepers by purchasing honey and beeswax products.
  • Avoid or reduce pesticide use in your garden[5]
  • Advocate for policies that protect pollinator habitats and reduce harmful agricultural practices.

Fun Facts

Honey bees perform a "waggle dance" to communicate the location of food sources.

Did you know that honey bees perform a "waggle dance" to communicate the location of food sources? This behavior enhances their efficiency in pollination[6].

The average worker bee produces about one-twelfth of a teaspoon of honey in its lifetime!
